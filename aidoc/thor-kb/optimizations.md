# Thor Optimization Backlog

> **Note**: This backlog mixes confirmed engine hotspots with workload-derived leads from [../../devdoc/thoropt.md](../../devdoc/thoropt.md). It is grouped by cross-cutting engine patterns rather than by activity family. Directly linked bullets are source-backed hotspots; unlinked bullets are workload-driven leads or heuristics.
>
> **Current ranking bias**: the representative production environment discussed so far is roughly 100, 200, or 400 workers with about 4 GB of row memory, 5 CPUs, and 200 GB of local disk per worker. Although many jobs are small, the bulk of runtime and cost sit in the largest graphs, including billion-row jobs and graphs with tens of thousands of activities. That means the highest-value items are the ones that cut row volume before explosive stages, preserve distribution, improve spill timing, or reduce graph-wide replay and coordination.

## 1. Hard Memory Walls and No-Spill Paths
- **No-spill exceptions need to be treated as a first-class watchlist**: Thor usually spills under pressure, but several important paths are intentionally whole-scope and all-memory. These are OOM-before-spill risks, not normal spill-tuning candidates.
- **`FILTERGROUP` still requires all-memory whole-group validation**: [../../thorlcr/activities/filter/thfilterslave.cpp#L258](../../thorlcr/activities/filter/thfilterslave.cpp#L258), [../../thorlcr/activities/filter/thfilterslave.cpp#L298](../../thorlcr/activities/filter/thfilterslave.cpp#L298), and the source note at [../../thorlcr/activities/filter/thfilterslave.cpp#L309](../../thorlcr/activities/filter/thfilterslave.cpp#L309) show that the helper contract forces `rc_allMem` group materialization before acceptance can be decided.
- **`DEDUP,ALL` is still an all-memory algorithm**: [../../thorlcr/activities/rollup/throllupslave.cpp#L88](../../thorlcr/activities/rollup/throllupslave.cpp#L88), [../../thorlcr/activities/rollup/throllupslave.cpp#L111](../../thorlcr/activities/rollup/throllupslave.cpp#L111), and [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1858](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1858) show that the helper materializes the whole scope through `rc_allMem`, and the shared collector explicitly disables spilling for that mode.
- **`ROLLUP GROUP` has the same no-spill whole-group materialization pattern**: [../../thorlcr/activities/rollup/throllupslave.cpp#L554](../../thorlcr/activities/rollup/throllupslave.cpp#L554), [../../thorlcr/activities/rollup/throllupslave.cpp#L574](../../thorlcr/activities/rollup/throllupslave.cpp#L574), and [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1858](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1858) load one full group through the same all-memory row-loader mode before calling the helper transform.
- **BEST-mode hash dedup keeps bucket output unspillable**: [../../thorlcr/activities/hashdistrib/thhashdistribslave.cpp#L3092](../../thorlcr/activities/hashdistrib/thhashdistribslave.cpp#L3092) explicitly notes that the returned stream should really be spillable. Under memory pressure, this is a confirmed blocker to further relief.
- **Selfjoin still buffers whole equal-key groups and only warns once they are already large**: `SelfJoinHelper` loads one full equal-key group into `curgroup` in [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L1017](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L1017) and records the resulting skew only when the group is already built at [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L1244](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L1244) and [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L1245](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L1245).

## 3. Distribution, Skew, and Centralized Coordination
- **Small-RHS join optimization**: investigate join paths where the RHS cardinality is extremely small. A lookup-style path may be much cheaper when semantics allow it.
- **Preserve distribution when possible**: avoid repartitioning work that is already local or can remain local.
- **Skew detection and mitigation**: self-joins, keyed joins, and grouped workloads can create massive skew that dominates elapsed time.
- **Single-node merge hot spots**: cases where distribute and merge work collapse onto one node need special scrutiny.
- **Local join starts the secondary side before it can prove the primary is empty**: [../../thorlcr/activities/join/thjoinslave.cpp#L264](../../thorlcr/activities/join/thjoinslave.cpp#L264) through [../../thorlcr/activities/join/thjoinslave.cpp#L307](../../thorlcr/activities/join/thjoinslave.cpp#L307) start the secondary input and may install lookahead before the local path can later discard it for empty-LHS inner joins at [../../thorlcr/activities/join/thjoinslave.cpp#L464](../../thorlcr/activities/join/thjoinslave.cpp#L464).
- **Local join still walks RHS-only groups even when unmatched RHS rows are irrelevant**: the helper enters `JSrightgrouponly` after each completed match group at [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L952](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L952) through [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L999](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L999), and the code explicitly notes that inner and left-only joins should avoid that extra pass at [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L989](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L989) through [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L994](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L994).
- **Local join continues scanning trailing RHS after LHS EOF even when right-outer semantics are not needed**: the helper explicitly marks this missed optimization at [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L928](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L928) through [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L931](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thsortu.cpp#L931).
- **Distributed join misses the local empty-side shortcut**: the local path can discard the RHS for empty-LHS inner joins at [../../thorlcr/activities/join/thjoinslave.cpp#L464](../../thorlcr/activities/join/thjoinslave.cpp#L464), but the global path still proceeds into secondary gather after detecting zero primary rows at [../../thorlcr/activities/join/thjoinslave.cpp#L582](../../thorlcr/activities/join/thjoinslave.cpp#L582) through [../../thorlcr/activities/join/thjoinslave.cpp#L589](../../thorlcr/activities/join/thjoinslave.cpp#L589).
- **Lookup join repeats RHS hash work across multiple stages**: the source already flags wasted hash calls in `processRHSRows()` at [../../thorlcr/activities/lookupjoin/thlookupjoinslave.cpp#L1115](../../thorlcr/activities/lookupjoin/thlookupjoinslave.cpp#L1115), and the same RHS rows are hashed again during local pruning, channel redistribution, keep-local filtering, and HT insertion.
- **Keyed join remote replies still serialize explicit `fpos` vectors**: both remote reply paths in [../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L1737](../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L1737) and [../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L1759](../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L1759) carry separate `fpos` payloads, and the code itself flags this as wasted serialization work.
- **Keyed join ordered mode pays dense per-part RHS bucket allocation**: [../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L3503](../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L3503) and [../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L3504](../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L3504) allocate RHS bucket arrays sized to `totalIndexParts` for each join group, even when actual fan-out is sparse.
- **Keyed join memory-pressure relief is $O(n)$ in queued groups**: `freeBufferedRows()` rescans the done and pending lists to recompute in-use bytes and spill candidates at [../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L3689](../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L3689), [../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L3713](../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L3713), and [../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L3789](../../thorlcr/activities/keyedjoin/thkeyedjoinslave.cpp#L3789).
- **Global `COUNTPROJECT` always installs a spill-enabled 12 MB lookahead before prefix-count transformation**: [../../thorlcr/activities/countproject/thcountprojectslave.cpp#L123](../../thorlcr/activities/countproject/thcountprojectslave.cpp#L123) through [../../thorlcr/activities/countproject/thcountprojectslave.cpp#L156](../../thorlcr/activities/countproject/thcountprojectslave.cpp#L156) install row-stream lookahead with `COUNTPROJECT_SMART_BUFFER_SIZE`, defined as 12 MB in [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thbufdef.hpp#L33](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thbufdef.hpp#L33), before the rank-to-rank prefix-count handshake.
- **`CHOOSESETS LAST` and `CHOOSESETS ENTH` impose a full-input pre-count barrier**: `ChooseSetsPlusActivity` installs lookahead over a counting wrapper with an explicit "read all input" note at [../../thorlcr/activities/choosesets/thchoosesetsslave.cpp#L279](../../thorlcr/activities/choosesets/thchoosesetsslave.cpp#L279), then waits for master-supplied totals before `LAST` or `ENTH` selection starts at [../../thorlcr/activities/choosesets/thchoosesetsslave.cpp#L358](../../thorlcr/activities/choosesets/thchoosesetsslave.cpp#L358) and [../../thorlcr/activities/choosesets/thchoosesetsslave.cpp#L443](../../thorlcr/activities/choosesets/thchoosesetsslave.cpp#L443).
- **Child evaluation is serialized per graph instance**: `CGraphBase::evaluate()` in [../../thorlcr/graph/thgraph.cpp#L2248](../../thorlcr/graph/thgraph.cpp#L2248) serializes child-graph evaluation for a graph instance.
- **`concurrentSubGraphs` is a memory-pressure lever**: [../../thorlcr/graph/thgraph.cpp#L2612](../../thorlcr/graph/thgraph.cpp#L2612) shows the async scheduler boundary. Raising graph overlap can improve throughput, but it also overlaps more spill-heavy activities on the same row-manager budget.
- **Global iterate and process are serial across slaves**: the inter-rank chain is explicit in `getFirst()` and `putNext()` at [../../thorlcr/activities/iterate/thiterateslave.cpp#L39](../../thorlcr/activities/iterate/thiterateslave.cpp#L39) through [../../thorlcr/activities/iterate/thiterateslave.cpp#L76](../../thorlcr/activities/iterate/thiterateslave.cpp#L76), and the source comment already says the global path is serial at [../../thorlcr/activities/iterate/thiterateslave.cpp#L78](../../thorlcr/activities/iterate/thiterateslave.cpp#L78).
- **Global loops pay a master-mediated sync round trip on every iteration**: slaves send iteration state in [../../thorlcr/activities/loop/thloopslave.cpp#L46](../../thorlcr/activities/loop/thloopslave.cpp#L46) and may block waiting for the master reply at [../../thorlcr/activities/loop/thloopslave.cpp#L61](../../thorlcr/activities/loop/thloopslave.cpp#L61), while the master waits for all slaves in [../../thorlcr/activities/loop/thloop.cpp#L41](../../thorlcr/activities/loop/thloop.cpp#L41) and only emits the "Still waiting" log at [../../thorlcr/activities/loop/thloop.cpp#L57](../../thorlcr/activities/loop/thloop.cpp#L57).
- **Non-local row `SOAPCALL` and `HTTPCALL` modes are intentionally first-node only**: non-local, non-grouped row call and row action only start helpers on `firstNode()` in [../../thorlcr/activities/soapcall/thsoapcallslave.cpp#L61](../../thorlcr/activities/soapcall/thsoapcallslave.cpp#L61) and [../../thorlcr/activities/soapcall/thsoapcallslave.cpp#L255](../../thorlcr/activities/soapcall/thsoapcallslave.cpp#L255).
- **Ordered funnel in child queries**: ordered funnels sourced from child datasets appear to be an expensive pattern and should be traced through graph and spill behavior.

## 4. Shared Spill Infrastructure, Setup, and Hot-Loop Costs
- **Grouped sort efficiency**: [../../devdoc/thoropt.md](../../devdoc/thoropt.md) calls out grouped sorts being materially slower than equivalent local sorts.
- **LocalSort is the clean loader baseline**: [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thgroupsortslave.cpp#L52](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thgroupsortslave.cpp#L52) and [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thgroupsortslave.cpp#L67](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thgroupsortslave.cpp#L67) show the simplest high-value consumer of `IThorRowLoader`.
- **Grouped local sort resets the loader per group**: [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thgroupsortslave.cpp#L97](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thgroupsortslave.cpp#L97) reloads every group via `loadGroup()`, and the default reset path in [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.hpp#L560](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.hpp#L560) and [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1846](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1846) discards row, spill, and counter state each time.
- **Grouped local sort rebuilds stream handoff machinery for each group**: the grouped `nextRow()` path in [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thgroupsortslave.cpp#L97](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thgroupsortslave.cpp#L97) repeatedly re-enters the `getStream()` handoff in [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1735](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1735).
- **MiniSort still resorts already sorted local data on the primary**: the small-data fast path in [../../thorlcr/msort/tsorts.cpp#L482](../../thorlcr/msort/tsorts.cpp#L482), [../../thorlcr/msort/tsorts.cpp#L526](../../thorlcr/msort/tsorts.cpp#L526), and [../../thorlcr/msort/tsorts.cpp#L560](../../thorlcr/msort/tsorts.cpp#L560) explicitly note that the primary resorts combined data instead of merging presorted runs.
- **Overflow partition correction is linear in duplicate-heavy spill regions**: `AdjustOverflow()` in [../../thorlcr/msort/tsorts.cpp#L788](../../thorlcr/msort/tsorts.cpp#L788) walks backward row by row across spill data until it finds a key below the split boundary.
- **Distributed sorter reuse is missing across activity reset**: [../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thmsortslave.cpp#L167](../../thorlcr/activities/msort/thmsortslave.cpp#L167) recreates the sorter and already documents that reuse would be better.
- **BEST-mode hash dedup should drain resident buckets before spilled buckets**: the source calls this out directly at [../../thorlcr/activities/hashdistrib/thhashdistribslave.cpp#L3076](../../thorlcr/activities/hashdistrib/thhashdistribslave.cpp#L3076). That ordering would free live memory earlier.
- **Global hash dedup still carries a hot-path lock because of async stopInput**: [../../thorlcr/activities/hashdistrib/thhashdistribslave.cpp#L3942](../../thorlcr/activities/hashdistrib/thhashdistribslave.cpp#L3942) documents that the current design forces a critical section around input fetches.
- **Callback ordering and ownership transfer are correctness-critical**: preserve the distinction between registered and activated spill callbacks in [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L156](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L156) and during `CThorRowCollectorBase::getStream()` handoff in [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1735](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1735).
- **Shared-reader spill callback cost is real overhead**: `CThorSpillableRowArray::save()` in [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1394](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1394) copies and sorts write-position callbacks and flushes at callback boundaries.
- **Deferred callback activation is intentional**: the delayed shared-callback installation note in [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1787](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1787) is a concrete deadlock-avoidance rule, not a style detail.
- **Staging-buffer and fragmentation tuning deserve measurement**: `CSpillableStream` in [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L425](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L425) uses a fixed 500-row staging buffer.
- **Grouped loader reuse**: look for opportunities to reuse row-pointer capacity across repeated groups instead of rebuilding spill-aware stream state each time.
- **TopN precedent**: `TopNSlaveActivity` already uses `clearRows()` to preserve high-water allocation state for repeated grouped work.
- **Row-stream setup cost**: measure the impact of spill callback registration and fixed `readRows` allocation before changing algorithms.
- **Tiny-group buffering overhead**: for workloads with huge numbers of tiny groups, `CSpillableStream` setup and spill-aware row buffering may cost more than the useful work.
- **Global iterate and process still use the wrong smart-buffer tuning knob**: the shared global start path installs lookahead with `ENTH_SMART_BUFFER_SIZE` at [../../thorlcr/activities/iterate/thiterateslave.cpp#L78](../../thorlcr/activities/iterate/thiterateslave.cpp#L78) through [../../thorlcr/activities/iterate/thiterateslave.cpp#L85](../../thorlcr/activities/iterate/thiterateslave.cpp#L85), even though Thor defines dedicated `ITERATE_SMART_BUFFER_SIZE` and `PROCESS_SMART_BUFFER_SIZE` in [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thbufdef.hpp#L11](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thbufdef.hpp#L11) through [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thbufdef.hpp#L14](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thbufdef.hpp#L14) and already advertises iterate memory with `ITERATE_SMART_BUFFER_SIZE` at [../../ecl/hqlcpp/hqlresource.cpp#L1879](../../ecl/hqlcpp/hqlresource.cpp#L1879) through [../../ecl/hqlcpp/hqlresource.cpp#L1882](../../ecl/hqlcpp/hqlresource.cpp#L1882).

The ungrouped code even marks the path with `better TBD?`.

## 5. Storage, Source-Path, and External I/O Setup Costs
- **Spill granularity is batch-based, not row-by-row**: `CThorSpillableRowArray::save()` in [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1394](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1394) only writes committed rows, while `commitDelta` in [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.hpp#L412](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.hpp#L412) leaves a small writer-owned tail temporarily unspillable.
- **Overflow policy can force later tails to disk**: `CThorRowCollectorBase::getStream()` in [../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1735](../../thorlcr/thorutil/thmem.cpp#L1735) may switch to all-disk or mixed output depending on `RowCollectorSpillFlags` and whether any earlier overflow already happened.

## 6. Observability and Timing Gaps
- **Missing total times**: activity chains such as `PROJECT` can hide the real cost when total-time accounting is incomplete.
- **Function-level timing holes**: helper or library code without timing attributes can distort graph-level diagnosis.
- **Suspicious activity timings**: review cases where distribute and funnel timing appear inconsistent with input and output volumes.
- **Local spill observability**: spill size and spill cost reporting appear incomplete; fix instrumentation before drawing strong optimization conclusions.

## 7. Graph-Shaping and ECL-Facing Opportunities
- **Early reduction before explosive stages is a first-class optimization target**: in graph-expansion workloads such as transitive-closure-style flows, reducing or spilling 100M rows before a self join can be far more valuable than optimizing how Thor later handles 100B rows after fanout.
- **Surface stronger feedback when a join could be rewritten as a `LOOKUP` join**: slow-job evidence already includes joins that appear to be tiny-RHS or distribution-preserving `LOOKUP` candidates.
- **Preserve distribution rather than falling into keyed or single-node patterns**: slow-job evidence includes keyed joins apparently fed from earlier joins and data apparently being read from a single node into a very large keyed join. Those cases deserve both engine scrutiny and user-facing feedback.
- **Add graph-shape guidance for early `MANY` or dedup reductions**: current slow-job notes repeatedly point at `MANY` or dedup before hash aggregate, distribute, selfjoin, and funnel stages as hour-scale savings.
- **Treat ordered funnel from child datasets as a rewrite candidate**: ordered funnel sourced from child datasets is already a known bad pattern in child queries and should be documented as a graph-shape warning, not just a funnel implementation hotspot.
- **Consider splitting one logical activity into multiple stages when it improves spill timing**: if separating a stage allows Thor to reduce, spill, or repartition a much smaller intermediate before a fanout stage, that is worth documenting even when the current graph generator does not do it automatically.
- **Flag expensive computed helpers when they dominate join cost**: slow-job notes highlight heavy SALT/helper functions and missing timing attributes, so user feedback should cover both rewrite candidates and poor observability in helper-heavy graphs.
